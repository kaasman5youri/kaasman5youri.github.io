    Summary

    The poem is an autobiographical sonnet. Milton describes his feelings about his experience with blindness. He thinks that he cannot serve God without his sight, but as the poem continues, he starts to believe that even though he is blind, God wants that he keeps on working.

    Analasis On his Blindness – John Milton (1652-1655)

    When I consider how my light is spent

    Pun:

    “When I consider how my light is spent”.

    Ere half my days in this dark world and wide,

    “world and wide.”.

    And that one talent which is death to hide

    Lodg'd with me useless, though my soul more bent

    To serve therewith my Maker, and present

    My true account, lest he returning chide,

    "Doth God exact day-labour, light denied?"
