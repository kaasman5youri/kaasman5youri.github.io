    Summary

    This piece is about a lost and failed love ("the smile on your mouth was the deadest thing ) . The speaker memorizes a winter day with his lover. He describes the scenery, but all he can remember are sad and neutral tones, like 'winter', 'grayisch leaves', because of his experience.

    Analasis Neutral Tones-Thomas Hardy (1867)

    We stood by a pond that winter day,

    Symbolism:

    Winter symbolizes melancholy and sadness.

    And the sun was white, as though chidden of God

    And a few leaves lay on the starving sod;

    – They had fallen from an ash, and were gray.

    Your eyes on me were as eyes that rove

    Over tedious riddles of years ago;

    And some words played between us to and fro.

    On which lost the more by our love.

    Enjambment:

    "And some words played between us to and fro On which lost the more by our love."
