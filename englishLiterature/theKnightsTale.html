    Intro

    The Knight’s Tale This story is written by Geoffrey Chaucer and is one story of the 24 completed stories in the Canterbury Tales. The knight is a noble man, of highest social standing, and so he tells an epic story of a chivalric romance. Rhyme Scheme: AA-BB-CC-…. (Only part I will be analysed for literary devices)

    Summary part1:

    Introduction of duke Theseus, Theseus was the lord of Athens. He was married to the queen Hippolyta. Emily was Hippolyta`s younger sister. Theseus was going home and came across a group of women, clothed in black, who complained that they lost their husbands in battle. Theseus promises that he will take revenge and defeat Creon, the lord of Thebes Theseus defeates king Creon. While pillaging Theseus and his fellows find two young knights, Arcita and Palamon. Palamon and Arcita where captured and imprisoned. Arcita wants a relationship with Emily and compares her with Venus (godess of beauty), but Palamon says that he already loves Emily. The quarrel, Arcita says that he loved Emily first and Palomon claimed he was first. Duke Pirithous, the friend of duke Theseus, knew Arcita and asked if he could be released. Arcita was released but he is not allowed to be in the country of Theseus. Arcita regrets losing sight on Emily, while Palamon pities himself still being in prison. The Knight asks which is the (un)luckiest...
