    Alisoun Anonymous late 13th or early 14th century song about the person's love for a woman named Alisoun. He only cares for this woman and the rest is irrelevant. Rhyme Sheme: in the stanza ABABBBBC and in the refrain DDDC

    Analasis Alisoun – Anonymous (14th century BC)

    Between March and April

    When the blossom1 begins to appear2

    The small birds take pleasure

    in singing in their language

    I live in love-longing

    Alliteration:

    Alliteration is prominent in this song. “I live in love-longing”.

    For the fairest of all things

    She is able to bring me bliss

    Alliteration:

    "She is able to bring me bliss".

    I am in her power

    “From all women my love is removed” “From alle wommen my love is lent,”.

    “In color, her hair is sufficiently fair” “On hew hire heer is fair ynough,”.

    Alliteration:

    “Her eyebrows are brown, her eyes are black” “Hire browe browne, hire yën blake;”.
